About

JAMHacks is Waterloo's first high school hackathon. On April 9th, come join 100 other high school students for 12 hours of creativity, innovation and fun!

FAQ

What is JAMHacks?

JAMHacks is a hackathon—a 12-hour-long "innovation marathon." You will work in teams of up to 4 to create an app, game, website, robot, or whatever your heart desires.

Who can participate?

JAMHacks is open to any high school student in Waterloo Region. If you're from elsewhere in Ontario and still want to come, send us an email at hello@jamhacks.ca. We'll work something out!

What if I don't know how to code?

No problem! A hackathon is a great place to pick up new skills. We'll have workshops and mentors to guide you from your first line of code to writing your own app or website!

Is there any cost?

Nope! The event will be free for all participants.

What should I bring?

You should bring your laptop (we will only be able to provide a very limited number of computers), phone, chargers, and some form of photo ID (students cards are fine). We will provide food, snacks and drinks!

Is there a code of conduct?

Yes. In short, be respectful to other people. You can read the full version here.

I have another question.

Feel free to send us an email at hello@jamhacks.ca.
